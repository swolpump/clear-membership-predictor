Methodology & Key Findings

Why External-Only: The previous model used CLEAR Revenue as a predictor, but revenue is essentially driven by membership count — making it a circular input. This updated dashboard uses only publicly available, forward-looking indicators that could actually signal membership changes before they happen.

Best Composite: Domestic Enplanements + Google Trends + Airline Revenue Index + Seasonality (Ridge Regression). LOO CV R² = 0.9708, MAPE = 6.04%. This outperforms the old CLEAR Revenue model while using only external data.

Google Trends Only: Google Trends "CLEAR airport" + Seasonality achieves R² = 0.9492, MAPE = 8.04% — remarkably strong for a single free indicator. This suggests search interest is a powerful leading signal for membership demand.

Key Insight: Combining travel volume metrics (enplanements, airline revenue) with consumer search intent (Google Trends) captures both the supply side (how many people are flying) and demand side (how many people are actively looking into CLEAR). The seasonal component captures Q2/Q3 peak travel effects.

Risk Factors: TSA's free touchless ID rollout could cannibalize CLEAR+ value proposition. Gross dollar retention declined from 88.5% to 86.4% in 2025. Credit card travel spending turned negative in H2 2025. The Google Trends model may overfit to search-sentiment cycles.

Growth Outlook: Both models project membership above 7.8M through 2026. The composite model sees a more moderate trajectory (8.0–8.2M range) while Google Trends suggests a tighter band around 7.6–7.8M. The divergence reflects different sensitivities to the travel recovery cycle.
